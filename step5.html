STEP 5: Scrolling & Game States

In Step 4 we learned how to get our character to use the platforms. Now let's focus on getting the character to jump higher than the top limit of the screen so that when our character hits a super platform when it is high on the screen, it will not stop at the top of the window and give us an error message. To pull this off, we will use a little trick common to moveable games such as this - we will have our character freeze in place and move everything down rapidly so that it looks like our character is going up. A tiny bit of cheating, I know, but it works great.

When a platform moves outside the screen, we want to generate another one on top of it but not in the exact same place because we need to maintain the programmed distance between the platforms. So we first need to calculate how far the platform travels below our game window and subtract that value from the total height of the window and generate the platform on that Y with a random X. Yes, it will pop up suddenly in the middle of the screen, but the player's attention will be focused on the character, so they will not notice that anything has happened (hopefully).
